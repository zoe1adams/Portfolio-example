5 Lessons I Learned as a Medical Scribe

Published: Jan 20, 2025 | Clinical Experience

Working in the emergency department as a medical scribe was one of the most eye-opening experiences in my pre-health journey. Here's what I learned:

1. Efficiency is Everything

Physicians are constantly multitasking. Learning how to document quickly and accurately was key to keeping up.

2. Medical Jargon is a Language

From abbreviations to lab terminology, I was immersed in medical language from day one.

3. Compassion Comes in Many Forms

I saw physicians show care through small gestures, clear communication, and patience—even under pressure.

4. Teamwork is Essential

The ER thrives on collaboration. Nurses, techs, clerks, and doctors rely on each other more than most people realize.

5. Every Case is a Learning Opportunity

Whether it was a common cold or a trauma activation, every shift brought something new to learn.
